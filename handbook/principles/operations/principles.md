### Handling internal product requests

_Surfaced while drafting [Evidence over Assumptions](../team/principles-list/evidence-over-assumptions.md)._

Internal product requests (from leadership, PM, engineers, design) arrive
often and feel free, safe, and fast — which silently fills the
work queue with opinion-driven work ahead of evidence-driven work.
The team principles ([Evidence over Assumptions](../team/principles-list/evidence-over-assumptions.md),
[Small Experiments over Large Builds](../team/principles-list/small-experiments-over-large-builds.md))
say what counts as evidence and how to generate it; they do **not**
say how internal requests should flow into the work queue. That's an
operations question.

Open sub-questions:

- Should internal requests be treated as **hypotheses** by default —
  parked in a "hypothesis backlog" — and graduate to build only after
  passing _Evidence over Assumptions_ (typically via a _Small
  Experiments_-sized experiment)?
- What's the team's experiment-slot capacity per quarter, and how are
  hypotheses chosen to fill it?
- What's the default response when a request has no behavioral signal
  and no plausible test?
- How do we record decisions so the same idea doesn't return six
  months later with no memory of prior evidence?
