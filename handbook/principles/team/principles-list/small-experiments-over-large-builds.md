# Small Experiments over Large Builds

We prioritize small, testable pilots over fully built solutions.
Speed of learning matters more than completeness.

## Decision checks

1. **Smallest version.** What is the smallest version we can put in front of real users within 4 weeks?

## Why it exists

1. We have spent multi-year cycles building toward "perfect" versions of products with no real-user feedback loop, and the result has been zero adoption — the cost of being wrong scales with how much we built before learning we were wrong.
2. The longer the build, the more expensive it is to discover the underlying assumption was wrong (see [Evidence over Assumptions](./evidence-over-assumptions.md)). Small experiments cap the size of that loss.
3. Polish and completeness do not unlock adoption (see [User Adoption over Features](./user-adoption-over-features.md)). If a small version doesn't get used, a complete version won't either — so building the complete version first wastes the bet.
4. This principle protects against the failure pattern of treating a not-yet-validated idea as a known-good roadmap to be executed.
