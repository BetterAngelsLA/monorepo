# 5. Outcome Metrics over Proxy Metrics

> **Status: aspirational.** This principle is accepted as true, but cannot
> currently fail a decision because no product has named its outcome
> metric. Until that changes, decisions cite it directionally but it is
> not a gate. See [Enforceability](#enforceability) below.

We prioritize measuring real-world outcomes over measuring activity or
data collection.
Activity is only valuable if it improves outcomes.

- _Decision check:_ Name the outcome metric this is meant to move, and how it will be measured.
- _Status:_ aspirational.

## Enforceability

We do not currently use real outcome metrics to make product decisions.
That is a gap, not a reason to weaken the principle. The principle stays
as written; what changes is its enforcement status.

**What "aspirational" means here:**

- A decision can pass this check today by naming an _intended_ outcome
  metric, even if it isn't being measured yet.
- The principle is a directional check, not a gate.
- Decisions made under it should be revisited once the metric is real.

**What would make it enforceable** (tracked as a follow-up):

1. Each active product names its outcome metric in writing.
2. Each metric has a measurement plan (data source, cadence, owner) — or
   a named proxy with a stated outcome it predicts and a date by which
   the predictive link will be checked.
3. The team agrees on the metric so different people answering the
   decision check arrive at the same answer.

Until all three hold, treat #5 as aspirational. Do not weaken the
decision check to make it pass — fix the underlying gap instead.

## Open questions

- Some outcomes take 6+ months to show up. How do we apply this principle
  to short-term decisions where the outcome can't be measured in time?
  (Likely answer: validated leading indicators — proxies that have been
  shown to predict the outcome — are acceptable.)
