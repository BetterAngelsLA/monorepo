Outreach App — Active Context

- Scope: Product (Outreach App)

- Situation Type: Product Phase — Customer Acquisition

- Principle Tradeoffs

  Only principles whose default weighting changes under this context are
  listed. The others apply normally.

  - Takes priority:
    - **Scalable Solutions over Individual Customers' Needs.** With
      only one customer, the gravitational pull is to build whatever
      they ask for. Under this context, that pull is wrong: we need
      to design for the broader population we have not yet landed.
      This wins against feature requests from the existing customer
      that don't transfer.
  - Takes lower priority:
    - **User Adoption over Features.** Still applies, but its
      standard signal (MAU growth among the existing customer's
      volunteers) is misleading here — user growth comes from the
      customer's own recruiting, not from us. Customer-side adoption
      (landing the next org) is the binding metric under this context.

- Justification

  1. Only one customer org; product feedback comes from a single
     source. Evidence: customer count has stayed at 1 across multiple
     outreach attempts in the last [N] months; no documented case of a
     second org moving from interest to commitment.
  2. No repeatable path to landing additional customer orgs — we have
     not yet identified what makes a prospective org say yes.
     Evidence: prior wins came from individual relationships, not a
     defined target profile of which orgs are likely to adopt or a
     conversion path we can reproduce.
  3. End-user acquisition is gated by the customer org, not by us.
     Evidence: user counts within the existing customer have grown, but
     that growth is attributable to the customer's volunteer recruiting,
     not to our product or marketing — meaning usage metrics overstate
     product-market signal.

- Duration: Open — runs until exit criteria are met.

- Exit Criteria

  - At least 2 additional customer orgs (3 total) in active use, OR
  - A documented, repeatable acquisition motion: a defined target
    profile of which orgs are likely to adopt, a predictable
    conversion path, and at least one new org closed using that motion
    end-to-end.
  - Whichever comes first ends this context. We then re-evaluate whether
    the next phase is User Adoption (driving usage within those orgs) or
    a different Product Phase.
