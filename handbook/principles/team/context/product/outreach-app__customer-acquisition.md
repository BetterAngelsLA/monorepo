Outreach App — Active Context

- Scope: Product (Outreach App)

- Situation Type: Product Phase — Customer Acquisition

- Principle Tradeoffs

  - Takes priority:
    - **Scalable Solutions over Individual Customers' Needs.** With
      only one customer, the gravitational pull is to build whatever
      they ask for. Under this context, that pull is wrong: we need
      to design for the broader population we have not yet landed.
      This wins against feature requests from the existing customer
      that don't transfer.
    - **Evidence over Assumptions.** We don't yet have behavioral
      evidence of what makes a prospective org commit. Work that
      generates that evidence (probes, structured pitches, recorded
      objections) wins against work based on what we assume orgs
      want.
    - **Small Experiments over Large Builds.** When we don't know
      what wins orgs over, run small tests. Wins against multi-month
      builds on the acquisition surface (demos, onboarding,
      integration story).
  - Takes lower priority:
    - **User Adoption over Features.** The principle still holds, but
      its standard signal (MAU growth in the existing customer) is
      misleading here — user growth comes from the customer's
      volunteer recruiting, not from us. Customer-side adoption
      (landing the next org) is the binding metric under this context.
    - **Real Constraints over Ideal Design.** Still applies; we just
      have fewer external-system constraints biting on customer
      acquisition than on the products with government/partner
      dependencies.

- Justification

  1. Only one paying customer; revenue and product feedback both come
     from a single source. Evidence: customer count has stayed at 1
     across multiple outreach attempts in the last [N] months; no
     documented case of a second org moving from interest to commitment.
  2. No repeatable path to landing additional customer orgs — we have
     not yet identified what makes a prospective org say yes. Evidence:
     prior wins came from individual relationships, not a defined ICP or
     conversion path we can reproduce.
  3. End-user acquisition is gated by the customer org, not by us.
     Evidence: user counts within the existing customer have grown, but
     that growth is attributable to the customer's volunteer recruiting,
     not to our product or marketing — meaning usage metrics overstate
     product-market signal.

- Duration: Open — runs until exit criteria are met.

- Exit Criteria

  - At least 2 additional customer orgs (3 total) in active paid use, OR
  - A documented, repeatable acquisition motion: a defined ICP, a
    predictable conversion path, and at least one new org closed using
    that motion end-to-end.
  - Whichever comes first ends this context. We then re-evaluate whether
    the next phase is User Adoption (driving usage within those orgs) or
    a different Product Phase.
