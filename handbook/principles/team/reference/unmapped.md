# Unmapped reflections and guiding questions

The items below are from the reflection notes but
**do not map cleanly to any current principle**. Each is annotated with why
it's left out — either because it's situational (belongs in
[context](./context.md), not principles), already implied by an existing
principle, or genuinely missing and worth a team discussion.

## Unmapped reflection assumptions

- **Outreach 1.2 — TBD:** "svc providers and governments want to understand their data."
  - _Why unmapped:_ This is an unvalidated **assumption about Customer demand**, not a tradeoff. If validated, it would inform context (e.g., "Acquisition phase, constraint = no customer pull") rather than a principle.
- **Outreach 1.3 — invalidated:** "orgs want to share their data."
  - _Why unmapped:_ Same as 1.2 — a Customer-behavior assumption. Its invalidation is a useful input to context, but doesn't suggest a universal principle.
- **Shelter 2.2 — TBD:** "we have to do this our way, from scratch, no collabs."
  - _Now mapped:_ Reframed as principle #8 [Scalable Solutions over Individual Customers' Needs](../principles-list/08-scalable-solutions-over-individual-customers-needs.md). The whether-to-partner question remains situational (context), but the underlying tradeoff is now captured as a principle.

## Unmapped guiding questions

- **#1: "right feature?"**
  - _Why unmapped:_ Too generic. Every principle is implicitly answering this. Not a tradeoff on its own.
- **#9: "can this work with a partner?"**
  - _Now partially mapped:_ The collaboration / multi-Customer aspect is covered by principle #8 [Scalable Solutions over Individual Customers' Needs](../principles-list/08-scalable-solutions-over-individual-customers-needs.md). Whether to engage a specific partner remains situational ([context](./context.md)).
- **#12: "is this user interested in tracking and improving outcomes?"**
  - _Now mapped:_ Re-read as an incentive question, this is covered by principle #9 [Aligned Incentives over Stated Needs](../principles-list/09-aligned-incentives-over-stated-needs.md). The question is really asking whether the user has an active incentive to engage with outcome tracking — not whether outcome metrics matter (which is #5).
- **#14: "clear R&R" (roles & responsibilities)**
  - _Why unmapped:_ This is a **team operations** concern, not a product decision tradeoff. Better placed in a separate operations doc.
- **#15: "aligned leadership"**
  - _Why unmapped:_ This is about **internal coordination**, not how to prioritize work. Out of scope for principles.
- **#17: "what opportunities and problems will it create?"**
  - _Why unmapped:_ Generic risk-analysis question, not a forced tradeoff. Could be folded into the rules around principle #2 (Validated Learning) but doesn't justify its own principle.
- **#19: "does someone else already do this better?"**
  - _Now partially mapped:_ Where this question is really asking "is this work generalizable / does it duplicate something useful elsewhere," it's covered by principle #8 [Scalable Solutions over Individual Customers' Needs](../principles-list/08-scalable-solutions-over-individual-customers-needs.md). Build-vs-buy specifics remain situational.
- **#20: "will this raise the profile of the org or provide funding opportunities?"**
  - _Why unmapped:_ This is **org-level fundraising / visibility strategy**, not a product tradeoff. We don't currently have a principle that tells us whether org-profile or funding considerations should weigh into product decisions — so we can't answer it. Keeping it here until we decide.
  - _Follow-up questions before this could become a principle:_
    - When (if ever) is it acceptable to prioritize work for visibility / funding over user value?
    - If a funder requires a specific feature or report, does that override principle #1 (User Adoption over Features), or is it handled as [context](./context.md)?
    - Should funding-driven work be tagged or tracked separately so we can audit how often it overrides other principles?
