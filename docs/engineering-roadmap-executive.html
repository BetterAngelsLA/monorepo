Engineering Roadmap — July–October 2026

For: CTO & CEO · July 21, 2026

What We're Doing and Why

We have two products in the field — the outreach app (field work, client tracking, service delivery) and the shelter operator portal (beds, availability, placements). SELAH is willing to trial a placement workflow if we can find shelters for their clients. SBCCOG wants to trial the outreach app — and is a potential placement partner downstream.

The next 3 months are about closing the loop: building the placement layer that connects partner demand to shelter supply, putting the portal through its paces internally while we grow the shelter side of the equation, and completing the platform cleanup that makes cross-organization work reliable.

We're deliberately not layering new features onto the outreach app. Mood tracking, deduplication, and similar enhancements don't block our ability to scale. What does: HMIS access for partners and finding more non-HMIS partners. The placement system is where new value gets created — not by adding fields to an app that already works.

The 3-Month Arc

Month 1 — Prep the System (July 21 – August 18)

This month is about dogfooding our own system, setting up interested demo partners, and kicking off the placement pilots. We move the data team onto the operator portal and retire the old admin. We onboard SBCCOG as a new outreach partner. And we begin the work that makes placement possible: helping SELAH find shelters, defining the client-to-shelter matching logic, and laying the groundwork for the placement system.

Operator Portal. Today our shelter data lives in a legacy Django admin that only BA staff can use. We're moving the data team onto the operator portal — the same tool shelters will eventually use — and retiring the old admin. This isn't just a migration: using the portal daily surfaces bugs and gaps we'd never catch in testing. Every rough edge we find now is one a shelter partner won't hit later.

Outreach App. SBCCOG is ready to trial the outreach app, so Month 1 starts with scoping their onboarding — what do they need, what's their workflow? Meanwhile SELAH has been using the app in the field and has reported bugs and feature requests. We're clearing those, adding search by DOB and phone number (a recurring ask), and improving how tasks surface to users. The July 24 SELAH meeting is our chance to demo the HMIS workflow and explore them as a placement partner — they're willing to trial if we can provide the shelter supply side.

Placement System. The placement system doesn't exist yet, but we know the shape of the problem: we can filter shelters by attributes today, but there's no systematic mapping between a client's profile and a shelter's requirements. Month 1 is about defining that mapping — what fields match, what data are we missing, what does the logic look like? In parallel, we're supporting Elissa and the Booz Allen volunteers on their ShelterConnect experiments, which gives us a live testing ground for placement ideas. And we begin scoping the placement pilot with SELAH: helping them find shelters for their clients.

Platform Cleanup. The teams migration has been partially done for months — a working system running alongside the old one through a temporary translation layer. We're removing that layer, dropping the old database columns, and cleaning up all references. We're also fixing client merge bugs that add friction to the internal team's daily work. It's the kind of work that doesn't ship features but makes every future feature faster to build and every internal workflow smoother.

Month 2 — Open to Partners (August 18 – September 15)

The theme this month is opening the system to the people who will actually use it. We've prepped the foundation — now we connect demand to supply and let partners in.

Placement System. With the client-to-shelter mapping defined and the portal data team supplying real shelter data, we can run our first placement pilot. SELAH's field team will try matching their clients to available shelters through the portal, while we continue supporting ShelterConnect experiments with Elissa and Booz Allen. Two parallel tracks means we're learning from both a structured pilot and real-world field use. SBCCOG is focused on outreach onboarding first; placement with them is a conversation for later.

Operator Portal. Now that the data team is using the portal daily, we add the layer that makes it valuable to shelters directly: a reporting dashboard showing vacancy outcomes, utilization trends, and placement metrics. Shelters can see the impact of posting their availability — which is what motivates them to keep doing it.

Outreach App. SBCCOG onboarding goes live — they're actively using the app for field work. We ship features based on what we learned from SELAH in Month 1. And we begin work on the permanent Liquid Glass fix: our current iOS workaround relies on an Apple compatibility flag that could disappear in any update, which would block us from future Expo SDK upgrades and security patches.

Platform Cleanup. We finish the permission migration across all remaining modules, and bring the mobile app up to parity with the admin portal — both now understand which organization you're working in. This is the work that makes cross-organization placement possible: SELAH's data is SELAH's, a shelter's data is the shelter's, and the placement system can bridge them securely.

Month 3 — Prove It Works (September 15 – October 13)

The theme this month is evidence. We've built the system and opened it to partners — now we measure whether it actually moves people into shelter.

Placement System. The target is 20 clients placed through the pipeline. We're onboarding 5+ shelters to manage beds through the portal, expanding the supply side so matching has more options. Cross-shelter coordination means if one shelter can't accept, the system automatically suggests alternatives — closing the loop from intake to placement without manual phone calls.

Outreach App. We hit the 10% service delivery growth target, driven by placements through ShelterConnect and SBCCOG coming online after onboarding. This is the number that shows the platform isn't just functioning — it's driving more services to more people.

Platform Cleanup. We evaluate whether our modern permission system can fully replace Django Guardian, the legacy third-party auth dependency. If yes, we plan the removal. If no, we document the gaps. Either way, we have a decision. We also run a code health sprint to clean up accumulated TODOs from months of fast-moving development — the kind of work that prevents future slowdowns. Between larger items, we can fit in small shelter directory improvements (map sizing, image sizing, desktop polish) — low effort, and by Month 3 there will actually be users to benefit from them.
